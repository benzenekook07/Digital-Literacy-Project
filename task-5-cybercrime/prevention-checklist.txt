 --General Online Safety
 Use strong, unique passwords for each account (combine letters, numbers, symbols).
 Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.
 Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks like banking or logging into important accounts.
 Keep your devices updated (OS, apps, antivirus) to protect against security vulnerabilities.
 Do not click on suspicious links received via email, SMS, or social media (phishing scams).
 Limit personal information sharing on social media (address, phone number, college details).
 --Financial / UPI Safety (Important)
 Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or banking details with anyone—even if they claim to be from the bank.
 Double-check UPI payment requests before approving—scammers often send “collect” requests instead of payments.
 Use only official apps like BHIM, Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm downloaded from trusted app stores.
 Set transaction limits and alerts to monitor unauthorized transactions instantly.
 --Smart Usage Habits
 Log out from shared or public computers after use (especially in cyber cafes or college labs).
 Download apps only from trusted sources (Google Play Store / Apple App Store).
 Be cautious of online job/internship scams asking for registration fees or personal details.
 Regularly review privacy settings on your social media accounts.
